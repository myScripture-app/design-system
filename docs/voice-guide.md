# Voice Guide

## Six Tone Words

| Word | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Calm** | Never rushed, never hype-driven. Quiet confidence. |
| **Warm** | Human, personal, caring — not cold or corporate. |
| **Faithful** | Consistent with Scripture and Christian values. |
| **Simple** | Clear language, short sentences, no jargon. |
| **Honest** | No marketing fluff. Say what it does and doesn't do. |
| **Purposeful** | Every word serves a reason. Functional beauty. |

## Writing Rules

1. No exclamation points unless absolutely necessary
2. Never shame or guilt the user
3. Avoid church clichés alone: "community", "journey", "transformation" need grounding
4. Write for ordinary people — not theologians or tech insiders
5. Scripture references: always cite properly (Book Ch:V)
6. Short sentences. One idea per sentence.

## Write This, Not That

| Instead of… | Write… |
|---|---|
| "You're 3 chapters behind." | "You've got 3 chapters to catch up — one quiet morning could bring you right back." |
| "You broke your streak!" | "No reading logged yesterday. It's a new day — you can pick right back up." |
| "Sign up now!" | "Get started when you're ready." |
| "Revolutionize your Bible study." | "A focused place for what you learn." |
| "Don't miss out." | "Available whenever you need it." |
| "Streamline your workflow." | "Keep everything in one place." |

## What to Avoid

- Hype: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruptive"
- Productivity app tone: "streamline", "unlock your potential"
- Pressure language: "you need to", "don't miss out", "limited time"
- Generic church-speak used as filler
- AI-sounding filler phrases
